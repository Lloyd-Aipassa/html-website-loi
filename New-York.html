New York

New York City

New York is the largest and most important city in the United States of America. About ten million people live in New York and just over twenty-two million in the metropolitan area. This puts New York in the list of the ten largest cities in the world. In many ways New York is one of the most important cities in the world, be it in the field of commerce, finance, art or fashion; New York is 'The place to be'. When you walk around Manhattan you notice everything that you are in the center of the world. The energy you feel when you walk through Manhattan is unlike any other city in the world.


Manhattan is just one of the five boroughs that make up New York. The others are Brooklyn, Queens, Staten Island and The Bronx. The other boroughs are much less recognizable, but are therefore no less worth a visit. A visit to New York is an accumulation of highlights for most tourists.

History

Manhattan Island and other parts of present-day New York were originally inhabited by the Lenni-Lenape Indians. The first European known to have come into contact with these Indians was Giovanni da Verrazzano. A bridge between Brooklyn and Staten Island is named after this explorer. It would be more than eighty years before another European sailed to what is now New York. This was the explorer Henry Hudson, who discovered the island of Manhattan. The same Hudson that took the Dutch to present-day New York, after which they built a few trading posts. The first settlements were built in 1624 and New Amsterdam was founded a few years later. In 1664 this colony was conquered by the English who, thanks to the Peace of Westminster, finally took control of this area. They renamed the city New York in honor of the Duke of York. After the War of Independence, New York became the capital of the then formed United States in 1788, until 1790 when Philadelphia received this honor. After this, things move quickly with New York, the New York Stock Exchange is opened in 1792, the Commissioners' Plan is introduced in 1811 and Central Park is constructed in 1857. Partly due to the Great Migration and the many immigrants seeking refuge in America, New York, with more than 10 million inhabitants, grew into the first megacity in the world at the beginning of the twentieth century. During this period, the skyline of the city is formed. Many of the skyscrapers that can be found in New York today were built in the 1920s and 1930s. In the rest of the twentieth century, the city experienced several peaks and valleys, but continued to grow steadily. In 2001, disaster struck the city when a terrorist attack was committed on the Twin Towers.

NY State of mind

Billy Joel already sang about it in the seventies and in 2009 Jay-Z and Alicia Keys did it again with 'Empire State of Mind'. When you walk in New York you feel the city, you notice everything that you are in a metropolis. The city is bustling and gives you energy. In that respect, the nickname “The City That Never Sleeps” certainly rings true. Walking through New York you realize at many times that you are in perhaps the most important city in the world. You will probably not let go of this feeling during a visit to New York. To understand what the New York State of Mind is, you must have visited New York at least once.

Nicknames

New York has many nicknames. The city's most famous nickname is probably "The Big Apple". The city owes this nickname to reporter John Fitzgerald who frequently used this nickname. The worldwide fame of this nickname is due to the 'I ♥ NY' advertising campaign that used an apple during their campaign. Another well-known New York nickname is “The City That Never Sleeps”. This nickname owes its fame mainly to the movie "New York, New York" with the song of the same name sung by Frank Sinatra. Another well-known nickname is “The Empire City”, which is mainly used to indicate the size of New York.

Top Attractions in New York City

New York City's top attractions include the Empire State Building, Central Park, Statue of Liberty, Metropolitan Museum of Art, and Broadway shows. These iconic landmarks and cultural institutions offer something for everyone and are not to be missed.

The Empire State is a landmark skyscraper in New York City, United States. It was completed in 1931 and has 102 floors, making it the tallest building in the world at the time. It is located in Midtown Manhattan and has become a symbol of the city, appearing in numerous movies and TV shows. The building is now a popular tourist attraction, with an observation deck on the 86th floor offering views of the city.

Central Park is a large public park in New York City, United States. It is located in the center of Manhattan and covers an area of 843 acres. Central Park is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, with numerous attractions including the Central Park Zoo, the Belvedere Castle, and the Strawberry Fields memorial to John Lennon. The park is also home to a variety of recreational activities such as running, biking, and boating.

The Statue of Liberty is a iconic symbol of freedom and democracy, located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. The statue, a gift from France to the United States, was designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and completed in 1886. It stands at a height of 151 feet and represents the Roman goddess of freedom, Libertas. The Statue of Liberty is a popular tourist destination and is visited by millions of people every year.

Broadway is a street in New York City, United States, known for its theaters and entertainment venues. It is the oldest north-south main thoroughfare in the city and stretches from Lower Manhattan to the Bronx. Many of the theaters on Broadway host plays and musicals, and the street is known as the "Great White Way" because of the many illuminated billboards and advertisements that line it. Broadway is a major tourist destination and is home to some of the most famous theaters in the world.

Where to Eat in New York City

New York City is home to some of the best restaurants in the world. From fine dining to street food, you'll find every type of cuisine imaginable in the city. Here are a few must-try restaurants:

Peter Luger Steakhouse is a fine dining restaurant located in Brooklyn, New York, United States. It is known for its high-quality steaks, which are dry-aged on the premises and grilled over a wood-fired grill. The restaurant has received numerous accolades, including being named the best steakhouse in the country by Zagat Survey and the James Beard Foundation. In addition to its steaks, Peter Luger Steakhouse is also known for its seafood, including lobster and oysters, as well as its extensive wine list. The restaurant has a casual atmosphere and is popular with tourists and locals alike.

Katz's Delicatessen is a New York City institution and a popular tourist destination, known for its classic deli sandwiches and traditional Jewish comfort food. The deli, which has been in business since 1888, is located on the Lower East Side of Manhattan and is known for its pastrami on rye, corned beef, and other cured meats. In addition to sandwiches, Katz's Delicatessen also serves a variety of side dishes, such as latkes, knishes, and kasha varnishkes, as well as soups and salads. The deli is also famous for its "I'll have what she's having" scene in the 1989 romantic comedy When Harry Met Sally.

Momofuku Noodle Bar is a popular restaurant in New York City, known for its innovative and delicious Asian-inspired cuisine. The restaurant, which was founded by chef David Chang, is located in the East Village neighborhood of Manhattan and is known for its noodles, dumplings, and other dishes. The menu at Momofuku Noodle Bar changes frequently and features a mix of traditional and modern dishes, such as pork buns, spicy chicken wings, and spicy tuna tartare. The restaurant has a casual atmosphere and is popular with foodies and locals alike.

Le Bernardin is a world-renowned fine dining seafood restaurant located in New York City. It is owned and operated by chef Eric Ripert and has received numerous accolades, including three Michelin stars and four stars from The New York Times. The restaurant is known for its innovative and sophisticated seafood dishes, which are prepared with the freshest and highest quality ingredients. Le Bernardin has a formal atmosphere and is a popular destination for special occasions and business dinners. It is located in Midtown Manhattan and has a sister restaurant in Paris.

Di Fara Pizza is a pizza restaurant located in the Brooklyn neighborhood of Midwood. It is known for its high-quality, wood-fired pizzas, which are made with fresh ingredients and topped with a variety of toppings. The restaurant was founded in 1964 by Italian immigrant Domenico De Marco, who is still the head pizza maker at the age of 87. Di Fara Pizza has a devoted following and has been praised by food critics for its delicious pizzas. The restaurant is small and often has a long wait for a table, but many customers believe that the pizzas are worth the wait.

Things to Do at Night in New York City

New York City is known for its nightlife, and there's always something going on no matter what day of the week it is. From live music to comedy clubs to rooftop bars, here are a few ways to enjoy the city after dark:

Broadway shows are live performances of plays, musicals, and other productions that take place in professional theaters in the Theater District of New York City. Broadway shows are known for their high-quality performances, talented casts, and elaborate sets and costumes. Many Broadway shows are based on popular films, books, or other works, and often feature well-known actors and actresses. Broadway shows are a popular tourist attraction in New York City and are attended by millions of people every year.

New York City is home to a wide variety of music venues, ranging from small clubs to large arenas. Some of the city's most well-known music venues include Madison Square Garden, the Radio City Music Hall, and the Barclays Center. These venues host a range of music acts, from pop and rock to hip hop and jazz. In addition to these larger venues, the city is also home to a number of smaller clubs and music venues, such as the Bowery Ballroom, the Mercury Lounge, and the Brooklyn Bowl. These smaller venues often feature up-and-coming artists and provide a more intimate concert experience.

New York City has a number of rooftop bars, which are popular destinations for tourists and locals alike. These bars, which are located on the top floors of hotels, restaurants, and other buildings, offer panoramic views of the city and a relaxed, upscale atmosphere. Some of the best rooftop bars in the city include The Roof at Park South, The Press Lounge, and The Top of the Strand. These bars often have a dress code and may require a reservation, so it is advisable to check in advance. Rooftop bars are especially popular in the warmer months, when patrons can enjoy the views and the outdoor atmosphere.

New York City is a mecca for stand-up comedy, with numerous comedy clubs and venues featuring stand-up performances. Some of the best stand-up shows in the city can be found at clubs such as the Comedy Cellar, the Gotham Comedy Club, and the Stand. These clubs feature a mix of established comedians and up-and-coming talent, and offer a variety of shows and formats, including open mics, improv, and sketch comedy. Other popular stand-up venues in the city include Carolines on Broadway and the UCB Theatre.

New York City has a vibrant nightclub scene, with numerous clubs and bars located throughout the city. These venues range from small, intimate clubs to large, extravagant dance clubs, and offer a wide variety of music and entertainment. The city is known for its diverse nightlife, and there are clubs and bars to suit all tastes, from hip hop and electronic dance music to jazz and live music. New York's nightclub scene is especially popular with tourists, who come from all over the world to experience the city's vibrant nightlife.
